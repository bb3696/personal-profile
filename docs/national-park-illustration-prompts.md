# National Parks Illustration Redraw Prompts

This document covers the 63 National Parks used by `src/data/parkList.js` and the corresponding image filenames generated by `src/utils/parkImages.js`.

No image filenames or references should be changed. Use each prompt to regenerate the existing image in place with the same filename.

## Universal Style Template

Create a horizontal modern 2D flat landscape illustration for a National Park travel tracker card. Use a cohesive national park poster style with clean geometric shapes, simplified natural forms, soft blue sky, low-saturation greens, warm gray rocks, sandstone orange cliffs, teal-blue water when present, and gentle layered depth. Preserve the park's recognizable landforms and visual identity while simplifying photographic texture, cluttered vegetation, harsh shadows, and tiny details. Use warm, clean, editorial travel illustration aesthetics inspired by classic national park posters, modern travel illustrations, Apple Maps-style simplification, and subtle Monument Valley-like shape clarity. No text, no people, no animals unless explicitly noted, no logo, no border, no 3D rendering, no oil painting, no anime, no cartoon character style, no photorealism, no HDR, no neon colors, no exaggerated gradients.

Recommended generation suffix for every prompt:

`Horizontal hero/card cover composition, 16:9 friendly, clear central landmark, balanced foreground/midground/background, consistent flat illustration style, low-saturation warm palette, crisp silhouettes, subtle paper-poster texture, no text.`

- Keep every replacement image horizontal and card-cover friendly.
- Use the same flat illustration language: simplified shapes, crisp silhouettes, no photorealistic texture.
- Maintain a shared palette: soft blue sky, muted forest green, warm gray rock, sandstone orange, teal-blue water.
- Preserve each park's primary recognizable landmark or landform.
- Avoid text, labels, borders, logos, people, and decorative UI elements inside the image.
- Avoid 3D rendering, oil painting, heavy gradients, anime/cartoon character styles, HDR contrast, neon colors, and overly detailed photo texture.
- Use similar shadow softness and detail density across all parks.
- Keep foreground/midground/background layering consistent.
- Generate replacements at the same aspect ratio and save them with the exact existing filenames.
- After replacing images, verify the National Parks grid on desktop, tablet, and mobile for consistent crop readability.
